## Our Pledge

We, the contributors and maintainers of Halal OS, pledge to make participation in our project and community a **respectful, welcoming, and harassment-free experience** for everyone — regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

Halal OS is rooted in **Islamic values of ihsan (excellence), adab (respect), and ukhuwwah (brotherhood/sisterhood)**. These values guide how we interact with one another in this community.

---

## Our Standards

### Encouraged Behavior ✅

- **Respectful communication** — Treat every person as you would want to be treated
- **Constructive feedback** — Critique code and ideas, never the person
- **Active listening** — Read carefully before responding
- **Gratitude** — Acknowledge the efforts of others
- **Patience** — Especially with newcomers and non-native English speakers
- **Assuming good intent** — If a message is ambiguous, ask before assuming
- **Helping others** — Answer questions, especially from beginners
- **Celebrating diversity** — Our Ummah spans 50+ countries and cultures

### Unacceptable Behavior ❌

- Harassment, bullying, or intimidation of any kind
- Sexualized language, imagery, or advances
- Personal attacks, insults, or derogatory comments
- Trolling, deliberate inflammatory posts, or bait
- Publishing private information (doxxing)
- Political or religious debate that is inflammatory or divisive (this is a tech project)
- Spam or self-promotional posts in inappropriate channels
- Discrimination based on race, nationality, language level, or school of thought (madhab)

### Special Notes for Our Community

- **Inter-madhab respect**: Halal OS serves the full Muslim Ummah — Sunni, Shia, and all Muslims. Do not use this space to debate sectarian issues.
- **Scholarly disagreement**: If you disagree with an Islamic feature implementation, engage respectfully and cite scholarly sources.
- **Non-Muslim contributors**: Non-Muslim contributors are fully welcome. You do not need to be Muslim to contribute.

---

## Our Responsibilities

**Project maintainers** are responsible for:
- Clarifying standards of acceptable behavior
- Taking appropriate and fair corrective action
- Removing, editing, or rejecting comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct
- Banning temporarily or permanently any contributor who violates these standards

Maintainers who do not follow or enforce this Code of Conduct in good faith may face repercussions as determined by the project leadership.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies:
- In all project spaces (GitHub Issues, Pull Requests, Discussions, Wiki)
- In the official Discord server
- When representing the project publicly (social media, conferences, blog posts)
- In private communications related to the project

---

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you observe or experience behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it via **one of the following**:

1. **Email**: conduct@halalos.org *(set up a conduct@ email alias)*
2. **Private message** to a maintainer on [Discord](https://discord.gg/halalos)
3. **GitHub**: Use the "Report Content" feature

All reports will be handled **confidentially**. We will:
- Acknowledge receipt within 48 hours
- Investigate thoroughly and fairly
- Protect the identity of the reporter
- Take appropriate action

### Enforcement Guidelines

| Violation Severity | Consequence |
|---|---|
| **Minor** (first offence, unintentional) | Private warning with explanation |
| **Moderate** (repeated or intentional) | Temporary restriction from participation |
| **Severe** (harassment, abuse) | Permanent ban from all project spaces |

### Appeals

If you believe a moderation action was unjust, you may appeal by emailing the same conduct address with your explanation. The decision of the maintainer panel is final.
